FAQs about Till Fellner

What is Till Fellner known for?

Till Fellner is renowned for his interpretations of baroque, classical, and romantic masterworks, especially pieces by composers such as J.S. Bach, Mozart, Beethoven, Schubert, and Liszt.

Where was he born?

He was born in Vienna, Austria.

Which prestigious competition did he win?

In 1993, he won first prize at the Clara Haskil International Piano Competition in Vevey, Switzerland.

With which orchestras has Till Fellner performed?

Fellner has performed with leading orchestras including the Berlin Philharmonic, Vienna Philharmonic, Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra, New York Philharmonic, and others.

Who were his teachers?

His musical education included studies with Helene Sedo-Stadler, Alfred Brendel, Meira Farkas, Oleg Maisenberg, and Claus-Christian Schuster.

Has he participated in any music festivals?

Yes, he has performed at numerous prestigious festivals such as the BBC Proms London, Salzburg Festival, and Mostly Mozart Festival New York, among others.

Is Till Fellner involved in chamber music?

Yes, he is an active chamber musician, collaborating with artists like the Belcea Quartet, tenor Mark Padmore, violinist Lisa Batiashvili, and cellist Adrian Brendel.

What are some highlights of his discography?

His discography includes works by Schubert, Schoenberg, Beethoven, J.S. Bach, and a recording of Beethoven’s Piano Concertos Nos. 4 and 5 with the Montreal Symphony under Kent Nagano.

Has he received any awards for his recordings?

In 2016, his recording of Brahms’ piano quintet with the Belcea Quartet received the Diapason d’Or de l’Année.

What educational role does Till Fellner hold?

Since autumn 2013, he has been teaching at the Zurich Hochschule der Künste.
